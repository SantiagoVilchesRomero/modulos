Nature   by   Issey Miyaki

This bathroom furniture piece, designed by Issey Miyake, draws its inspiration from the fluid beauty of nature. The organic lines mimic the soft curves of flowing water, with surfaces that seem to ripple gently like a stream. The materials blend natural wood with delicate stone, evoking the serenity of a forest or a riverbank.

Subtle textures on the cabinet doors resemble leaves or bark, adding a tactile element that invites touch. The design embodies a harmonious balance between simplicity and elegance, offering a peaceful, nature-infused atmosphere to any bathroom space.

The furniture's color palette reflects earthy tones, with soft greens, deep browns, and gentle grays, reminiscent of a tranquil woodland setting. Miyake’s attention to detail is evident in the way light interacts with the surfaces, creating subtle shadows that mimic sunlight filtering through trees.

Flat ID-8 designs and manufactures wooden furniture using timber sourced exclusively from sustainable forests. They prioritize eco-friendly practices, ensuring that each piece is crafted with minimal environmental impact.

Their wood suppliers adhere to strict reforestation guidelines, helping to preserve natural ecosystems while reducing deforestation. Flat ID-8 also focuses on long-lasting craftsmanship, producing high-quality furniture that reduces waste over time. Their commitment to sustainability reflects in both their materials and designs, promoting a greener future for the furniture industry.

Stone

Stone   by  
Yosida Murakami

The kitchen, bathroom, and living room furniture line by Yosida Murakami is inspired by the principles of Feng Shui and Asian traditions. Each piece is designed to promote balance and harmony in the home, using natural materials like bamboo wood, stone, and soft metals that connect with the five elements of Feng Shui: wood, fire, earth, metal, and water. The color palette is neutral and soft, with earthy tones that aim to create a calm and fluid atmosphere in every space.

In the kitchen, Murakami has integrated furniture that encourages smooth movement, with open cabinets and shelves that allow for easy energy flow. The sinks and work surfaces are positioned to avoid direct confrontation between water and fire elements, following Feng Shui guidelines closely. The arrangement of these pieces is meant to encourage organization and mental peace while cooking.

For the living room and bathroom, Murakami has chosen low furniture and clean lines, allowing energy to flow freely. In the living room, sofas are strategically placed to promote social interaction, while in the bathroom, the elements are balanced to offer a sense of calm and purification, incorporating details like round mirrors and soft lighting to evoke serenity and renewal.

Feng Shui brings harmony between people and space.

GEO

Yosida Murakami

Finishing / Bush-hammered

The *Geo* bathroom design is a serene, nature-inspired space that features natural stone as its centerpiece. The walls and floors are lined with smooth, polished stones in earthy tones, creating a grounded, organic atmosphere. A freestanding stone bathtub serves as the focal point, with a matte finish that blends seamlessly into the surrounding environment. The sink and countertop are carved from the same stone material, providing a cohesive, minimalist look.

Natural light floods the space through large windows, reflecting off the stone surfaces to enhance the room’s calming ambiance. Accents of greenery and soft lighting complete the *Geo* bathroom, evoking a tranquil, spa-like retreat rooted in the beauty of the natural world.
