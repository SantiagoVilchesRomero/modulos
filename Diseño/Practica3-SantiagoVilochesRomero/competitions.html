Cave Diving Competitions

The Eagle’s Nest Expedition (Florida, EE.UU.)

Known as ‘The Everest of cave diving’, Eagle's Nest is a submerged cave system in Florida that attracts some of the world's most experienced cave divers. The system is notorious for its extreme depths, narrow passages and challenging visibility conditions. These expeditions are not formal competitions, but exploration and study missions in which new areas of the caves are mapped, species are studied and findings are shared with the scientific and diving community.

The Mexican Cenote Exploration Project (Yucatán, México)

The cenotes of the Yucatan Peninsula are home to some of the most extensive and beautiful underwater cave systems in the world. Expeditions to the cenotes are conducted by experienced divers who are dedicated to exploring, mapping and documenting the cave networks. Divers from all over the world have participated in the project, and each year new routes and connections between caves are discovered. The aim is to preserve these natural spaces and to encourage scientific research into the unique ecosystems found in them.

Motocross Competitions

FIM Motocross World Championship

This motocross world championship, organised by the Fédération Internationale de Motocyclisme (FIM), is one of the most important motocross competitions worldwide. It takes place annually and consists of several rounds of races held in different countries. Each round has tracks with difficult and technical terrain, full of jumps and obstacles. Riders compete in different categories, and the championship is defined by the sum of points accumulated throughout the season. It is the stage where the best riders in the world face each other and show their motocross skills.

AMA Supercross Championship (EE.UU.)

Organised by the American Motorcyclist Association (AMA), AMA Supercross is a supercross competition, which is a variant of motocross. Races are held in stadiums and indoor arenas in different cities across the United States. The tracks are designed with jumps and tight turns, allowing spectators to see the action up close. The best supercross riders participate in this championship, which is considered one of the most prestigious in the world of motocross. Several rounds are held and riders score points that determine the championship.

Hang-gliding Competitions

FAI World Hang Gliding Championships

The World Hang Gliding Championship is the most important event in competitive hang gliding, organised by the Fédération Aéronautique Internationale (FAI). Every two years, pilots from around the world gather at a different venue to compete in a series of distance flying races. Events may include point-to-point flights, triangular courses or endurance tests, and pilots must plan their routes using thermal currents to maximise flight time. The championship is a showcase for the skill and strategy of the pilots, who must adapt to the flying conditions and plot efficient routes to win.

Red Bull X-Alps

The Red Bull X-Alps is one of the most extreme and challenging competitions in the world of adventure sports, combining hang-gliding (paragliding) and hiking through the Alps. Every two years, athletes must cross around 1,200 kilometres of alpine terrain, using hang gliding to cover long distances and walking or climbing when flying is not possible. The route crosses several countries, and competitors must plan their flight strategy and physical endurance in mountainous terrain. It is a test of strength, endurance and navigational skill.
